# Real User Experience Improvements

This folder is for the deeper product question:

> How does KitesForU become meaningfully easier, clearer, and more trustworthy to use, not just more polished?

The working stance here is deliberate:

- this is about real user outcomes, not surface cleanup
- the right unit of analysis is the user journey, not a page in isolation
- the goal is to reduce confusion, uncertainty, and wasted effort
- good ideas should map back to the real scenarios in the product, not generic UX taste

## Core product judgment

KitesForU does not mainly suffer from a lack of capability. It mainly suffers when the user has to do too much translation:

- translate their goal into the right route
- translate their role into the product's language
- translate a generation status into "what happens next"
- translate storage architecture into a learning or teaching workflow

The biggest UX wins will come from removing that translation burden.

## Research anchors

This package is grounded in:

- live walkthroughs on the beta product
- the scenario findings already captured on 2026-04-21
- the current business proposals under `business/features/`
- external UX / service-design guidance from GOV.UK, Nielsen Norman Group, Baymard, and Apple

The external sources are not used as decoration. They are used to stress-test product decisions against known user-behavior patterns:

- people follow the strongest information scent
- people prefer recognition over recall
- every extra decision competes with the user's actual goal
- journeys break when the service is organized around internal architecture instead of user intent
