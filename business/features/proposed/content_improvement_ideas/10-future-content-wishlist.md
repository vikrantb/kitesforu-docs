# Future Content Wishlist

This file is intentionally bold.

It lists adjacent or missing content opportunities that do not fit neatly inside the current scope but may be strategically valuable later.

## 1. Job Search and Career Narrative

This sits just next to interview prep, but it is a different job.

Potential areas:

- networking conversation prep
- recruiter screen prep
- "tell me about yourself" identity work
- resume story translation
- career-switch confidence rebuilding

## 2. Difficult Conversations

There is a broad set of human situations where people want to sound composed, clear, and credible:

- giving feedback
- receiving criticism
- asking for a raise
- managing conflict
- setting boundaries

This could connect strongly with both workplace and life-skill use cases.

## 3. Oral Exams, Viva, and Presentation Defense

Not quite interview prep, not quite exam prep.

Potential value:

- thesis defense support
- oral exam rehearsal
- board or leadership presentation defense
- investor Q&A preparation

## 4. Parent-Child or Family Learning Content

This is different from K-12 teacher content.

Potential jobs:

- explaining school concepts at home
- bedtime learning hybrids
- curiosity-led family audio
- parent confidence for homework support

## 5. Case-Based Professional Judgment

This could extend:

- law
- medicine
- business
- management
- public policy

The content focus would be:

- what would you do
- what are the trade-offs
- what is the hidden risk

## 6. Creator's Room / Story Development Aids

For storytelling and creative creators:

- premise testing
- character chemistry ladders
- reveal-order experiments
- alternative endings
- genre crossovers

## 7. Reflection and Debrief Content

Many users improve most after the event, not before it.

Potential jobs:

- post-interview debrief
- post-exam reflection
- post-training manager debrief
- post-lesson teacher reflection

## 8. Confidence and Recovery Tracks

There may be standalone value in content designed for:

- restarting after failure
- recovering from a bad interview
- getting back into studying after avoidance
- rebuilding momentum after embarrassment or confusion

## 9. Expert Briefings and Executive Synthesis

This is not just explainer podcasting.

It is a different job:

- "I need the sharpest 10-minute synthesis on this topic"
- "Tell me the implications, not the background"
- "Help me sound informed in a leadership conversation"

## 10. Multivoice Debate / Competing Lenses

Some topics become much better when framed as:

- two schools of thought
- two competing diagnoses
- one claim vs its strongest counterclaim

This could be useful for:

- business
- policy
- medicine
- history
- technology

## Wishlist thesis

The content surface may ultimately want to expand beyond "study, train, explain, and tell stories" into:

- prepare me
- coach me
- brief me
- help me decide
- help me recover
- help me lead a conversation

That expansion is not a tech question yet.
It is a scope and product-opportunity question.
