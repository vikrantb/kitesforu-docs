# Quality Rubrics
<!-- last_verified: 2026-02-07 -->

## Curriculum Quality (Syllabus Worker Output)

### Score 9-10 (Excellent)
- Every episode title is specific to the candidate's background and target role
- Gap analysis correctly identifies the 3 most critical gaps
- Episode progression follows logical skill-building order
- Practice questions are realistic and role-specific
- Episode types are correctly classified and varied

### Score 7-8 (Good)
- Most titles are specific, 1-2 may be slightly generic
- Gap analysis captures major gaps but may miss nuances
- Progression is logical but could be optimized
- Practice questions exist but may not be perfectly calibrated

### Score 5-6 (Mediocre)
- Mixed specific and generic titles
- Gap analysis is surface-level
- Progression is acceptable but not optimal
- Practice questions are generic

### Score 1-4 (Poor)
- Generic titles that could apply to anyone
- Gap analysis is missing or wrong
- No logical progression
- No practice questions or irrelevant ones

## Episode Content Quality (Podcast Worker Output)

### Score 9-10 (Excellent)
- References specific items from candidate's resume
- Includes company-specific context (recent news, culture, values)
- Host personas are distinct and maintained throughout
- Natural conversational flow, not scripted-sounding
- Actionable advice with concrete examples
- Pacing appropriate for audio consumption

### Score 7-8 (Good)
- Some resume references, mostly relevant
- General company context (not deeply researched)
- Host personas mostly consistent
- Good flow with minor awkward transitions
- Useful advice, some concrete examples

### Score 5-6 (Mediocre)
- Few or no resume references
- Generic company info (Wikipedia-level)
- Host personas blur together
- Stilted conversation, feels scripted
- Advice is textbook, not personalized

### Score 1-4 (Poor)
- No personalization at all
- Wrong or no company context
- Single voice or indistinguishable hosts
- Robotic, unnatural dialogue
- Generic advice available in any interview book

## Script Quality Standards
Based on shared quality guidelines in kitesforu-workers:
- **5-Second Rule**: Every 5 seconds must deliver a fact, insight, example, or meaningful question
- **Banned patterns**: "That's interesting", "Many people think", "It's growing", "Recently" (replace with specifics)
- **Information density**: Specific facts, statistics, examples â€” never vague statements
- **Anti-repetition**: Each segment must provide unique information

## Audio Quality Standards
- No TTS artifacts or stitching glitches
- Appropriate voice for persona
- Natural pacing with proper pauses
- Smooth transitions between segments
- Consistent volume throughout
