## What this is

The **Voice-First Mastery Engine** is the system KitesForU uses to turn every
voice interview you complete into personalized coaching on your next session.
The engine has three phases in its rollout, and **Voice Instrumentation** is
phase one:

1. **Voice Instrumentation (shadow logging)** — this page
2. **Live coaching** — the interviewer's follow-up questions adapt in real time
3. **Cross-session adaptation** — starting difficulty, tone, and focus area
   adjust to your recent history

Voice Instrumentation is the foundation all three phases sit on. It is the
pipe that turns each answered voice turn into a durable, tenant-scoped,
replayable event.

## What changes in your experience

**Nothing.** The whole point of shadow logging is that you cannot hear it.

- The interviewer's voice does not change
- The question flow does not change
- The feedback you see between turns does not change
- The latency of your voice turn does not change

If you hear a difference, something is wrong and you should file a support
ticket.

## What happens under the hood

<Steps>
  <Steps.Step title="You finish a voice turn">
    Silero VAD detects your silence, OpenAI's streaming transcriber returns
    your final transcript, and ElevenLabs Flash v2.5 starts speaking the
    interviewer's next question. No change to any of this.
  </Steps.Step>
  <Steps.Step title="The provisional picker runs in milliseconds">
    A deterministic, pure-function picker reads your transcript and the
    current question's canonical skill tags. In a handful of milliseconds
    it decides: "this answer is missing a number in the result," or "this
    answer is good, no drill needed."

    There is no LLM on this path. There is no database lookup on this path.
    The picker is **safe for the audio thread** — it never blocks, never
    waits on the network, never delays the next question.
  </Steps.Step>
  <Steps.Step title="The turn becomes an immutable event">
    KitesForU wraps the picker's decision in a queue envelope that carries:
    your tenant, org, and user identifiers; the session id; the turn id;
    the transcript; the question's skill tags; and the picker's reason
    code and confidence.

    The envelope is enqueued locally in the room process. A background
    task drains the local queue into Pub/Sub so the durable write never
    blocks your audio.
  </Steps.Step>
  <Steps.Step title="The room catches up if it opened stale">
    If your room opened while our projection cache was behind the event
    stream — for example, because you just finished a session two minutes
    ago and the rollup worker has not caught up yet — the room replays
    any pending queue envelopes into its in-memory snapshot before your
    first turn. You notice nothing. The system corrects itself.
  </Steps.Step>
  <Steps.Step title="Observation, not action">
    For now, the picker's decision is logged for our team to audit. It
    does not yet drive the interviewer's follow-up. That wiring ships in
    a later PR after we have observed ≥ 100 real voice turns and
    validated that the provisional picker agrees with our rubric judge
    at least 70% of the time.
  </Steps.Step>
</Steps>

## Why we ship shadow-first

Three reasons, in order of importance:

1. **Audio responsiveness is sacred.** Our voice turn budget is 900ms from
   your silence to the first TTS audio frame. Anything that might spend 20ms
   of that budget on a heuristic has to earn the spend. Shadow mode lets us
   measure the real cost before we take it.

2. **The picker's accuracy needs ground truth.** The picker is deterministic
   pattern-matching on your transcript. The real rubric judge is a much
   slower LLM call that produces the authoritative signal. We want to see
   how often the cheap picker agrees with the expensive judge on real
   sessions **before** we let the cheap picker drive interviewer behavior.

3. **Privacy and trust.** Every event we emit is tenant-scoped from the first
   byte. If your organization is on the enterprise tier, your voice event
   stream lives under a path prefix only your tenant can read, and the event
   payload itself carries redundant tenant/org/user identifiers so any
   downstream job can enforce isolation without trusting only the path.

## What you can do today

If you are using the voice interview preview, the answer is short:
**use it normally**. That is the ground truth we are calibrating against.

If you are an operator wanting to watch the logs: look for structured log
entries with the key `voice_mastery_shadow_pick` and the field
`reason_code`. The reason codes you will see in shadow mode are:

- `missing_quantified_impact` — the answer did not name a number in the result
- `missing_star_result` — the answer did not state an outcome
- `weak_ownership_language` — the answer used "we" more than "I"
- `carry_forward_quantified_impact` — a previous turn flagged this weakness and
  the current answer still has not addressed it
- `insufficient_signal` — the answer did not trigger any of the above; no drill
  would have been offered

## Opt-out and data retention

Voice Instrumentation writes to an immutable event log retained for 90 days.
After 90 days, raw events are folded into cross-session projections and the
original event documents are deleted. Projections retain numeric rollups
only, not raw transcripts.

If your organization has a GDPR or contractual requirement to delete all
voice events for a specific user, that cascade deletes the event log, the
projections, and the skill graph in one idempotent operation.

## When coaching goes live

The next PR in this chain (PR B) wires the picker into the interviewer's
follow-up voice. When that lands, you will see a micro-tooltip in the app
the first time you start a voice session in a newly instrumented room.
Until then, shadow mode is silent by design.
