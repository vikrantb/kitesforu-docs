## What this is

Every time you finish a voice answer, three fast things happen on the
audio thread:

1. The provisional picker decides, in under a millisecond, what kind of
   drill your answer *probably* needs — numbers, result, stage,
   ownership, specificity.
2. The picker's decision is wrapped in an immutable envelope and
   pushed to a queue.
3. Your interviewer's next sentence starts playing.

That is the complete story of the audio thread. It never waits for a
database read. It never waits for a model call. It never waits for a
slower "real" judge to second-guess the picker.

**Reconciliation and projection** is the slow, careful part of the
loop that runs entirely *off* the audio thread. It is what turns a
stream of fast provisional decisions into the durable, authoritative
record that shapes your next interview.

## Three pure functions, one async loop

<Steps>

### 1. The rubric judge reconciler

After your answer, a separate worker pulls the envelope off the queue
and runs an **authoritative rubric judge** — the slow, expensive
version of the picker that sees the full five-dimension rubric:
specificity, quantified impact, role-outcome clarity, ownership, and
level-appropriateness.

The judge may take several hundred milliseconds. It is allowed to call
language models. It is allowed to read your history. But none of that
touches the voice.

When the judge is done, the reconciler compares it to the picker's
earlier guess and produces one of four outcomes:

- **Confirmed** — the judge agrees with the picker. Same drill, same
  reason. The picker was right; move on.
- **Corrected** — the judge agrees the answer needs a drill but picks
  a different one. The Mastery Trace records the correction so
  analytics can see where the picker's heuristics disagree with the
  rubric.
- **Added** — the picker thought the answer was fine, but the judge
  found something worth coaching. A net-new drill is surfaced.
- **Dismissed** — the picker flagged something and the judge said
  no, it was fine. The provisional decision is quietly withdrawn.

The reconciler is a pure function. Same inputs, same outputs, every
time. It never blocks on I/O.

### 2. The projection fold

Once a turn is reconciled, its decision is folded into two per-user
projections:

- **DimensionLedger** — a per-skill rollup that tracks how many times
  a skill has been exposed, how many times it needed a drill, and a
  **recency-weighted** drill score. The score decays at 0.9 per
  exposure, which halves after about seven turns — fast enough to
  react when you start hitting a skill cleanly, slow enough that one
  lucky answer doesn't wipe out a real gap.

- **SkillGraph** — a ranked top-N view over the ledger. This is what
  the room loader reads when you join your next interview. It tells
  the picker which weaknesses are currently most load-bearing, so the
  picker's history-aware carry-forward check has something to work
  with.

Both projections are **rebuildable** from the turn trace log. If one
gets corrupted, the consumer replays the log and rebuilds it from
scratch. They are never the source of truth — only a fast view on top
of it.

### 3. Snapshot refresh

When you join your next interview, the control-plane loader takes the
SkillGraph for your user and folds it into the room snapshot — the
tiny, bounded blob of state the audio thread is allowed to read. The
refresh is a pure function too. It hydrates the snapshot's recent
weakness summary from the top-ranked skills in your graph, caps the
result at five entries, and marks the snapshot fresh.

By the time the audio thread looks at the snapshot, every projection
read has already happened — off-thread, before the room was marked
ready.

</Steps>

## Why this preserves the voice-first charter

<Callout type="warning">
  Mastery features shed before voice quality sheds. Always.
</Callout>

The voice-first charter has four rules. The reconciliation and
projection pipeline was designed explicitly around them:

| Rule | How this pipeline honours it |
| --- | --- |
| 1. Audio thread does nothing slow | All three functions are pure, run off-thread, and are never called from the audio path |
| 2. Bounded join-time snapshot only | The only thing the audio thread ever sees is a five-item `recent_weakness_summary` that was populated *before* the room was ready |
| 3. Async durability only | The reconciler and fold are the async pipeline — they *are* the durable handoff |
| 4. Mastery sheds before audio | If the consumer falls over, the voice keeps playing. The snapshot just comes in with an older weakness list next time. |

## Staleness is handled intentionally

A subtle but important rule: the snapshot refresh is a **no-op** when
the projection is older than the snapshot's last applied live
envelope. This happens when the shadow-logging queue has already
replayed fresh envelopes into the snapshot in memory, but the
projection hasn't caught up yet.

Refreshing in that case would overwrite fresh data with stale data,
so the refresh function returns the snapshot unchanged. The next
projection rebuild will catch up. This is the kind of invariant that
feels over-cautious until one day it saves a user's mock interview
from getting wiped.

## What you will notice as a user

Nothing, yet. Coaching is still silent. The reconciler is logging
confirmation / correction / addition / dismissal events to the
Mastery Trace so we can see how often the picker's fast heuristics
agree with the slower authoritative rubric. Once that agreement rate
clears the same audit bar picker v2 cleared (≥75%, ≥80% precision and
recall), the downstream coaching layer unlocks — and you will start
hearing the picker's strongest drills inline in your next interview.
